1. Anemia is a common condition. What happens when a person has anemia? You didn't answer this question.
The correct answer is B. The blood does not have enough red blood cells . These cells carry oxygen to all the cells in the body. Anemia isn't a single disease but a condition that has many different causes.

2. What is the most common cause of anemia?
The correct answer is C. Too little iron in the blood . This is the most common cause worldwide.

3. Which of these groups is the most likely to have anemia?
The correct answer is B. Women . Women ages 65 and younger are more likely to suffer from anemia than men.

4. How does anemia affect the body?
The correct answer is A. The blood doesn't deliver enough oxygen to the body . Severe anemia can affect heart function.

5. A lack of which of these will result in abnormally large red blood cells and a condition called megaloblastic anemia?
The correct answer is C. Vitamin B-12 and folic acid . Both are needed for red blood cells to grow to the right size and shape to carry oxygen.

6. Which of these are signs of anemia?
The correct answer is A. Pale gums . Pale fingernail beds and eyelid linings, forceful heartbeats, and fatigue may signal mild anemia.
